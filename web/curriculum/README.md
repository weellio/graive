# Prompt Engineering & Modern World — Kids' Online Course
### Ages 10–18 | Self-Paced | Video + Lesson Plan Format

---

## What This Course Is

A practical, hands-on online course that teaches kids how to think with AI, navigate the modern digital world, and build skills that matter now and in the future. Prompt engineering is the entry point — but the deeper goal is developing **human judgment amplified by AI**.

---

## Course Structure

| Tier | Ages | Theme | Modules |
|------|------|-------|---------|
| 🔭 Explorer | 10–11 | Talking to Computers | 6 modules |
| 🔨 Builder | 12–13 | Understanding the Machine | 6 modules |
| 🧠 Thinker | 14–15 | Critical AI Citizenship | 6 modules |
| 🚀 Innovator | 16–18 | Build with AI, Think Beyond AI | 6 modules |

---

## The 5 Pillars (woven through every tier)

1. **AI Literacy** — what it is, how it works, when to distrust it
2. **Information Discernment** — deepfakes, filter bubbles, verification
3. **Digital Economy** — attention, data, platforms, future of work
4. **Human Skills** — empathy, creativity, ethics (AI-proof skills)
5. **Systems Thinking** — feedback loops, unintended consequences

---

## Prompt Engineering Progression Across Tiers

```
Ages 10–11:  INTENTION   → "What do I actually want?"
Ages 12–13:  CLARITY     → "How do I explain this clearly with context?"
Ages 14–15:  STRUCTURE   → "How do I break this into steps and guide reasoning?"
Ages 16–18:  SYSTEMS     → "How do I chain prompts into automated workflows?"
```

---

## Each Module Contains

- **Video Script** — 5–10 minute engaging video for kids to watch
- **Lesson Plan** — self-guided activity the kid does after the video

### Video Script Structure
1. Hook — surprising question or live demo
2. Concept — simple analogy tied to something kids already know
3. Demo — show prompting in action on screen
4. Challenge — something to try right now
5. Recap + tease of next episode

### Lesson Plan Structure
- Objective (one clear skill)
- What you'll need (tools, free accounts)
- Watch first (video)
- Try it (step-by-step activity)
- Level up (harder challenge)
- Reflect (journaling questions)
- Share (optional community prompt)
