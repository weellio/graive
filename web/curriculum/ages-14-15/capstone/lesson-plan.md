# Capstone Project: The Position Paper
### Thinker Level (Ages 14–15) | Final Project

---

## What You're Building

A 500–800 word position paper on a real issue at the intersection of technology and society.

This project requires you to use AI *as a research tool* — not as a writer. The thinking, the argument, and the voice are yours.

---

## The Brief

**Choose a real issue. Research both sides rigorously. Write your own position.**

Suggested topics (or propose your own):
- Should AI-generated content require labelling?
- Should there be a minimum age for social media (and if so, what)?
- Should companies that automate jobs pay a "robot tax"?
- Should facial recognition be banned from public spaces?
- Should AI be allowed to make decisions in schools (e.g. exam grading, admissions)?
- Should AI-generated art be allowed to compete in art competitions?

---

## Step 1: Research the Issue (30 mins)

Use chain of thought prompting to research both sides:

**Prompt:**
```
I'm researching [YOUR ISSUE] for a position paper.
Work through this carefully:

Step 1: What are the key facts and context I need to understand?
Step 2: What are the 3 strongest arguments FOR [position A]?
Step 3: What are the 3 strongest arguments FOR [position B]?
Step 4: What evidence exists for each side? Where is evidence strong? Where is it weak?
Step 5: Who are the key stakeholders and what do they each want?
Step 6: What are the most important values in tension here?

Don't give me a conclusion yet. Just the research.
```

Verify 3–4 key facts from the AI's research with external sources.

---

## Step 2: Find Your Position (20 mins)

Now that you've heard both sides, where do you actually stand?

Answer these before writing:
1. Which argument did you find most persuasive and why?
2. Is there a position between the extremes? What would it look like?
3. What's the strongest objection to your view? How do you respond to it?
4. Are there things you're genuinely uncertain about?

Your position doesn't have to be absolute. "I think X, with these important caveats" is a strong position.

---

## Step 3: The Essay Scaffold (15 mins)

Use AI to help build your structure — *not your writing*:

```
I have a position on [ISSUE]: [YOUR POSITION IN ONE SENTENCE].
Help me structure a 500–800 word position paper by asking me:
1. What is my specific, arguable thesis?
2. What is my first supporting argument?
3. What is my second supporting argument?
4. What is the strongest counterargument to my view?
5. How do I respond to that counterargument?
6. What is my concluding point?
Ask me one at a time and push back if I'm being vague.
```

Write your answers. This is your outline.

---

## Step 4: Write the Paper (45 mins)

Write the paper yourself. No AI writing — only your voice.

Structure:
- **Introduction** (hook + thesis statement): ~100 words
- **Argument 1** with evidence: ~150 words
- **Argument 2** with evidence: ~150 words
- **Counterargument + response**: ~150 words
- **Conclusion** (restate position + broader significance): ~100 words

After you've written a draft, you may use AI for editing feedback:
```
Please give me feedback on this draft. Focus on:
1. Is my thesis clear and specific?
2. Does my evidence actually support my claims?
3. Is my counterargument response convincing?
4. Where am I being vague or underdeveloped?
Don't rewrite anything — just give me notes. [PASTE DRAFT]
```

Then revise based on your own judgement.

---

## Step 5: Reflect on the Process

Answer these after completing the paper:

1. Did your position change at all during the research process? If so, how?
2. What was the hardest part of this project?
3. How did using AI as a research tool differ from just using it to write the paper?
4. What would you need to know to be *more* confident in your position?

---

## Evaluation Criteria (Self-Assessment)

Rate yourself 1–5 on each:
- [ ] My thesis is specific and arguable (not just a topic)
- [ ] My evidence actually supports my claims
- [ ] I took the counterargument seriously
- [ ] My position is mine — not just the AI's
- [ ] I learned something I didn't know before starting
