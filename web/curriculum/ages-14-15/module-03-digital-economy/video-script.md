# Video Script: The Digital Economy
### Thinker Level (Ages 14–15) | Module 03 | ~9 minutes

---

## HOOK (0:00–0:45)

**[HOST on camera]**

"TikTok made $16 billion in revenue in 2023. Instagram — $32 billion. YouTube — $31 billion.

None of those platforms charge you to use them.

So where does all that money come from?

Spoiler: partly from advertisers. But the deeper answer is more interesting — and more relevant to your life than you might think.

Today we're following the money."

**[TITLE CARD: "The Digital Economy"]**

---

## THE ATTENTION ECONOMY (0:45–3:00)

**[HOST, animated]**

"The modern internet runs on what economists call the **attention economy**. The core idea: human attention is a finite, valuable resource. Platforms compete to capture as much of it as possible — because attention = ad revenue.

This shifts the fundamental product of tech companies. They're not selling you the app. They're selling your attention to advertisers.

And attention has a price. A targeted ad shown to someone likely to buy costs more than a generic one. So platforms are incentivised to know as much as possible about you — so they can charge more for access to your eyeballs.

The more they know, the more precisely they can target. The more precisely they can target, the more they can charge. The more they can charge, the more they invest in keeping you on the platform.

**[DIAGRAM: attention → data → targeting → revenue → more engagement investment → more attention]**

This is the machine. And most of what you see online — the algorithms, the recommendations, the notifications — are outputs of this machine."

---

## HOW AI CHANGED THE ECONOMY (3:00–5:00)

**[HOST]**

"AI has accelerated this in a few important ways.

**1. Content at scale**
Generating text, images, and video used to require human labour. Now it's cheap. Which means the internet is filling up with AI-generated content faster than humans can review it. Some of it is valuable. Some of it is designed purely to capture search traffic or ad clicks.

**2. Hyper-personalisation**
AI enables recommendation and targeting at a level humans couldn't manage manually. Your feed is uniquely yours — which sounds nice, but also means you're being optimised as a unique target.

**3. New kinds of value**
AI also creates genuinely new economic value. Productivity tools, medical diagnostics, scientific research, code generation — these aren't just about ads. There's a real productivity revolution happening in the economy.

The question is: who captures that value? The companies that build the AI? The workers who use it? Everyone equally? These are live economic and political questions."

---

## THE CREATOR ECONOMY (5:00–6:30)

**[HOST]**

"There's another piece of this that directly affects young people: the creator economy.

YouTube, TikTok, Substack, Patreon — these platforms allow individuals to build audiences and make money from them. A 16-year-old with a compelling YouTube channel can earn more than a teacher.

This is genuinely new. For most of human history, making a living as an individual creator was nearly impossible. Now it's a viable career path.

But it comes with caveats:
- You're building on someone else's platform — they can change the rules
- The revenue is concentrated: the top 1% of creators make the vast majority of money
- Algorithms decide your reach — and they can change without notice
- Mental health costs are real: the pressure to perform, the parasocial relationships, the public exposure

The creator economy is a genuine opportunity. But going in with clear eyes about the structure matters."

---

## YOUR ECONOMIC ROLE (6:30–7:30)

**[HOST, direct to camera]**

"Here's the part that's most directly relevant to you.

You are simultaneously:
- A **consumer** (using platforms)
- A **product** (your attention being sold)
- A **potential creator** (if you make content)
- A **future worker** (in an economy being reshaped by AI)

How you position yourself in that matrix matters.

If you understand the attention economy — you can choose when to engage and when to step back.
If you understand the creator economy — you can decide whether to participate and on what terms.
If you understand how AI is reshaping work — you can make better decisions about what skills to develop."

## CHALLENGE (7:30–8:15)

**[HOST]**

"This week's challenge is research-based.

Pick one major tech platform — YouTube, TikTok, Instagram, Spotify, whatever you use most.

Use AI to help you research: how does it actually make money? Who are its biggest revenue sources? How is AI changing its business model?

Then: who benefits most from this model? Who pays the real costs?

Write a one-page summary. Use AI for research, but write the analysis yourself."
