# Lesson Plan: Personal Brand & Digital Identity
### Thinker Level (Ages 14–15) | Module 06

---

### Part 2: The Audit of Your Social Presence (10 mins)

Pick your most active social platform. Scroll back through your last 30 posts/interactions.

Ask yourself honestly:
- Is there anything here I'd be embarrassed by in 5 years?
- Am I proud of anything here?
- Does this represent who I actually am or a version I was performing?

No need to share this — it's just for you. But note: is there anything you want to clean up?

---

### Part 3: The Digital Identity Vision (20 mins)

Use AI as a thinking partner to help you articulate what you actually want:

**Prompt:**
```
I want to think about my digital identity and online presence.
I'm 14-15 years old and interested in [YOUR INTERESTS — be specific].
Help me think through:
1. What would a genuine, useful online presence look like for someone with my interests?
2. What platforms would make most sense and why?
3. What could I create or share that would be genuinely useful to others?
4. What kind of reputation would I want to have built by the time I'm 18?
5. What are the risks I should be aware of?
```

Use its responses as a thinking prompt — don't just copy them. Build your own answers.

**Write your vision:**
- What I want to be known for by age 18: _______________________
- The platform(s) where I'll build that: _______________________
- The type of content/contribution I'll make: _______________________
- What I won't do / my personal rules: _______________________

---

### Part 4: The First Step (5 mins)

Identify one small, concrete thing you could do this week to start building toward that vision.

It could be:
- Writing a short piece about something you know
- Creating an account on a platform aligned with your goals
- Joining an online community around something you care about
- Documenting a project you're working on

## Level Up — The 5-Year LinkedIn Thought Experiment

Imagine you're 19 or 20, applying for a job, university place, or grant in something related to your current interests.

Write the "About" section of a LinkedIn profile or portfolio introduction that you'd want to have by then.

Use AI to help you:
```
I'm 15. I'm interested in [INTERESTS/GOALS]. Help me write an "About Me" draft for what I'd want my profile to say when I'm 19, as if I'd had 4 years to build a real presence and body of work in this area. What would the ideal version of my journey look like? Write it in first person as if I'm 19 looking back.
```

This is a vision document. Now: what would you need to do in the next 4 years to make it true?

## Reflect

1. Before this module, how consciously were you thinking about your digital footprint? What's changed?

2. What's the difference between "performing" a personal brand and genuinely building one? Can you give an example of each?

3. Is it possible to be authentic *and* strategic at the same time? Or does strategy undermine authenticity?
