# Thinker Level — Ages 14–15
## Theme: Critical AI Citizenship

---

### Who This Is For
14–15 year olds who've grown up online, probably use AI tools already, and are starting to think about bigger questions — identity, fairness, the future of work, what's real. They're ready for nuance and complexity.

### The Big Idea
Using AI isn't enough. You need to think *about* AI — who benefits, who's harmed, how it changes society, and where *you* stand. At the same time, you'll develop real advanced skills that will actually matter in school, university, and work.

### Core Skill by End of Tier
Use chain-of-thought prompting to break complex problems into steps, and critically evaluate AI outputs for bias, completeness, and fairness.

### Capstone
Research a real-world problem using AI, build an argument for *both* sides, then write a personal position paper explaining where you stand and why — using AI as a research tool throughout, not a ghostwriter.

---

### Prompt Engineering Progression: STRUCTURE
> "How do I break this into steps and guide the AI's reasoning, rather than just asking for an answer?"

Chain-of-thought prompting is the key skill here — asking AI to *show its work* and building prompts that unfold in stages.
