## What Is a Manifesto?

A manifesto is a public declaration of beliefs, values, and intentions. Famous manifestos have launched political movements, art movements, and revolutions.

Your AI manifesto is a personal document — it doesn't need to launch anything. But it should:
- Be genuinely yours (not AI-generated)
- Take clear positions, not just describe debates
- Show that you've thought carefully, not just summarised what you've read
- Acknowledge complexity and uncertainty where it's real
- Say what you are committed to doing, not just what you think

> "A manifesto isn't an essay. It doesn't just analyse. It takes a stand."

---

## Key Concepts: What a Good Manifesto Includes

### 1. A Statement of Values
What principles guide your thinking about AI? These might include:
- Human dignity and autonomy
- Fairness and non-discrimination
- Transparency and honesty
- Environmental responsibility
- Freedom from surveillance
- Access and equity

### 2. Beliefs About AI's Nature
What do you actually believe about what AI is — and isn't?
- Is AI creative? Conscious? Deserving of moral consideration?
- What are AI's most important limitations?
- What are its most powerful capabilities?

### 3. Position on Key Issues
Based on everything you've studied, what do you think about:
- AI and creativity / copyright
- AI surveillance
- AI's climate footprint
- AI and mental health / social media
- AI and the future of work
- AI bias and fairness

### 4. Commitments
What will you actually DO based on these beliefs?
- How will you use AI tools?
- What will you refuse to do with AI?
- How will you engage as a citizen with AI policy?
- What will you tell others?

### 5. Honest Uncertainties
A credible manifesto admits what you don't know. What questions are still genuinely open for you?

## The Writing Process

### Phase 1: Beliefs Inventory (15 mins)

Go back through your notes from the 12 Thinker modules. For each topic, write a one-sentence statement of what you actually believe:

Module 1 (Chain of Thought): ___
Module 2 (Ethics / Bias): ___
Module 3 (Digital Economy): ___
Module 4 (Jobs / Automation): ___
Module 5 (AI for Learning): ___
Module 6 (Digital Identity): ___
Module 7 (AI Creativity): ___
Module 8 (Surveillance): ___
Module 9 (Climate): ___
Module 10 (Mental Health): ___
Module 11 (Future of Work): ___

Now look at these beliefs. Are they consistent? Do any of them contradict each other? Note any tensions:

Tensions I notice: ___

---

### Phase 2: Core Values (10 mins)

From your beliefs inventory, identify the 3–5 core values that run through most of what you believe. These are your ethical foundations.

My core values on AI:
1. ___
2. ___
3. ___
4. ___
5. ___

Write a sentence for each explaining WHY this value matters to you specifically — not just because it's a good general principle, but what makes it personally important.

---

### Phase 3: Draft Your Manifesto (25 mins)

Now write your manifesto. It should be 400–700 words, in your own voice.

**Suggested structure:**

**Opening declaration** (2–3 sentences): Who are you in relation to AI? What is your fundamental stance?

**What I believe about AI's nature** (3–5 sentences): What IS AI — and what isn't it?

**What I think AI is doing to the world** (1–2 short paragraphs): What are the effects you're most concerned about, and most excited by?

**My positions on [2–3 specific issues you feel strongly about]**: Where do you stand, and why?

**What I commit to** (a list of 5–8 specific commitments): Not abstract principles — concrete actions or stances. For example:
- "I will not use AI to write work I submit as my own without disclosure"
- "I will challenge surveillance systems I encounter"
- "I will vote for politicians who take AI regulation seriously"
- "I will continue learning about how AI actually works, not just how it appears"

**What I'm still uncertain about**: One or two genuine open questions — showing intellectual honesty.

**Closing**: One sentence that captures your relationship with AI going forward.

---

### Phase 4: The AI Test (10 mins)

**Important rule for this phase:** You are allowed to use AI, but only to challenge and stress-test your manifesto — not to write or rewrite it.

Ask your AI:
1. "Here is my AI manifesto. What's the strongest argument against my main positions?"
2. "Are there any internal contradictions in what I've written?"
3. "What perspective or argument have I ignored that I should consider?"

Read the responses. Revise your manifesto if you find the feedback genuinely compelling.

Did you change anything based on AI's feedback? ___
Did you reject any of its suggestions? Why? ___

## Sharing Your Manifesto

Your manifesto is a living document. You can:
- Share it with people who disagree with you and have a conversation
- Post it publicly if you want to contribute to the broader discourse
- Revisit it in one year and see what's changed

Rate your current manifesto's completeness and confidence:
- Completeness: ___/10
- Confidence in my positions: ___/10
- Honesty about uncertainties: ___/10

## Challenge
**The Living Manifesto:**

Set a reminder to revisit this manifesto in exactly one year. Before you read it again, write a short "prediction" about which of your views will have changed, and why.

Then in a year:
- Compare your predictions to reality
- Update any positions that have shifted
- Add new positions on issues that didn't exist when you first wrote it
