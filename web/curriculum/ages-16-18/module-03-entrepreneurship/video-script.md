## THE AI LEVERAGE STACK (0:45–3:00)

**[HOST, diagram]**

"Think of AI as a leverage multiplier on whatever you already bring.

If you bring: domain knowledge + problem understanding + taste → AI can help you produce, communicate, research, and iterate at speed.

Here's what that leverage stack looks like in practice:

**Research & Analysis** → AI can scan, summarise, synthesise information at scale
**Writing & Communication** → emails, proposals, marketing copy, documentation
**Code & Technical Work** → debugging, generating boilerplate, explaining concepts (if you know some coding)
**Customer Research** → analysing feedback, generating survey questions, synthesising patterns
**Product Iteration** → generating options, stress-testing ideas, mapping edge cases

What AI is still bad at:
- Understanding a specific context deeply (you bring this)
- Knowing what customers actually want from lived experience (you bring this)
- Taking responsibility for outcomes (you bring this)
- Building trust and relationships (you bring this)

The formula: **Your unique insight + AI's leverage = unfair advantage.**"

## THREE BUSINESS MODELS TO KNOW (3:00–6:00)

**[HOST, concrete examples for each]**

"There are three business models that are especially interesting in the AI age:

**Model 1: Service businesses with AI leverage**
Traditional: a one-person writing service can produce maybe 5 pieces a week.
With AI: the same person can produce 20+ pieces, with AI doing drafts and them doing strategy, editing, and client relationships.

This isn't about replacing human quality — it's about removing the bottleneck on volume. Same applies to: design, research, translation, summarisation, content creation, customer support.

**Model 2: AI-powered productised services**
You take a repeatable workflow you've mastered and turn it into a product.
Example: 'I'll turn your podcast transcript into a newsletter, 5 social posts, and a blog article for £X/month.' — The workflow is almost entirely AI-assisted. You're selling the system, not your hours.

**Model 3: AI-native information products**
Courses, guides, tools, databases — content or utility that's valuable to a specific audience. AI accelerates creation. Your unique knowledge and taste is the moat.

In all three models: **your distribution and reputation matter more than ever.** AI commoditises production. Trust and audience are the scarce resource."

## THE IDEA FRAMEWORK (6:00–8:00)

**[HOST]**

"How do you find a real idea? Not a clever idea — a *real* one?

The most reliable framework: **find the intersection of three things:**

1. **A problem you personally understand** — because you've experienced it, or you know people who have
2. **A group of people who have that problem** — specific people, not 'everyone'
3. **A willingness to pay** — the problem is annoying enough that they'd pay to fix it

Notice: not 'a problem you think is interesting.' A problem other people have and hate enough to pay for.

AI tools are especially good for problems involving: information overload, repetitive processing, content creation, research aggregation, and personalisation.

Avoid: starting with 'let me use this cool AI tool' and working backwards. Start with the problem."

---

## VALIDATION BEFORE BUILDING (8:00–9:30)

**[HOST]**

"The single biggest mistake first-time builders make: spending months building before finding out if anyone wants what they're building.

The right order:
1. Describe the problem
2. Find 5-10 people who have the problem
3. Ask them about it (not: 'would you use this?' but 'tell me about the last time this was a problem for you')
4. Describe your solution concept to them: 'If I could [solve this problem] for you for [price], would you want that?'
5. If yes: ask them to pre-pay or sign up

If you can't get one person to pay before you've built anything — that's important information.

AI can help with steps 1-4: generating questions to ask, synthesising patterns from conversations, helping you articulate the problem more sharply."

---

## CHALLENGE (9:30–10:15)

**[HOST]**

"Your challenge: come up with one genuine product or service idea that uses AI as part of its delivery.

Requirements:
- It solves a specific problem for a specific type of person
- AI handles at least one step in the workflow
- You can describe it in one sentence: 'I help [WHO] do [WHAT] using [HOW]'
- You could theoretically validate it with no code at all

Use AI to stress-test your idea:
> 'Here is my business idea: [DESCRIBE IT]. Act as a sceptical but constructive investor. What are the three biggest risks? What would you need to see to believe this could work?'

Then: who are 5 real people in your life who might have this problem? How would you test it with them?"
