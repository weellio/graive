## CONCEPT: FROM PROMPTS TO SYSTEMS (0:45–2:30)

**[HOST]**

"There are three levels of prompting sophistication.

**Level 1: Single prompts** — You ask, AI answers. Simple. Useful. Limited.

**Level 2: Conversations** — You iterate in a single chat, refining outputs. More powerful.

**Level 3: Systems** — You design a sequence of prompts that work together. The output of one becomes the input of the next. You automate complex, multi-step tasks.

Most people operate at Level 1 or 2. Today we're going to Level 3.

The analogy: Level 1 is texting one friend for advice. Level 3 is running a meeting where different specialists contribute to a shared goal."

---

## TECHNIQUE 1: PROMPT CHAINING (2:30–5:00)

**[HOST, screen share]**

"Prompt chaining is when you break a complex task into sequential steps — and each step builds on the previous one.

Example: I want to write a compelling YouTube video script on a topic.

**Unchained (Level 1):**
> 'Write a YouTube script about how social media affects teen mental health.'
**[Show generic output]**

**Chained (Level 3):**

Step 1 — Research:
> 'Give me the 5 most well-supported findings from research on social media and teen mental health. Include the strongest evidence and the key debate points. Format: numbered list with one-sentence evidence summary each.'

Step 2 — Angle selection (I choose the angle based on Step 1's output):
> 'Based on finding #3 about social comparison effects, help me develop a unique angle for a YouTube video — one that's not just "social media is bad." I want something nuanced and surprising.'

Step 3 — Structure:
> 'Using this angle: [paste angle from Step 2], create a 5-part structure for a 10-minute YouTube video. Each section needs: a clear purpose, a key point, and a suggested visual or demo.'

Step 4 — Script:
> 'Write Section 1 in full using this structure. Tone: conversational, slightly provocative, aimed at 16-22 year olds. 150–200 words.'

**[Show the difference in quality and control]**

See how much better the output is — and how much more *control* you have at each step? You're not hoping the AI gives you what you need. You're directing it precisely."

## TECHNIQUE 2: META-PROMPTS (5:00–7:30)

**[HOST]**

"A meta-prompt is a prompt that helps you write better prompts. It's prompting *about* prompting.

Example: You have a task you need AI help with, but you're not sure how to frame it. Use this meta-prompt:

> 'I want to use AI to help me with this task: [describe your task briefly]. What is the best way to prompt you to get excellent results? Give me a recommended prompt structure with explanations for each element.'

**[Demo: Use this on a real task]**

The AI will essentially write a better prompt template for you — and explain why each part matters. Then you use that template.

Another powerful meta-prompt pattern: the critique loop.

> 'Review my prompt below and tell me: what's unclear? What context is missing? What constraints should I add? Then give me an improved version.
> My prompt: [paste your draft prompt]'

This is like having an experienced prompt engineer review your work before you run it."

---

## TECHNIQUE 3: REUSABLE TEMPLATES (7:30–9:30)

**[HOST]**

"The highest-leverage thing you can build is a reusable prompt template — a fill-in-the-blanks structure you can use for any instance of a repeated task.

Example — a weekly summary template:

```
WEEKLY CONTENT SUMMARY TEMPLATE

You are a clear, analytical assistant.
Context: I am summarising [TYPE OF CONTENT] from [TIME PERIOD].
Audience: [WHO THIS IS FOR]

Your task:
1. Extract the 3 most important points or developments
2. Identify one trend or pattern
3. Flag one thing that seems underreported or worth investigating
4. Give a one-sentence 'so what' conclusion

Content to summarise:
[PASTE CONTENT HERE]

Format: Structured, concise. Use headers. Under 300 words total.
```

Now every week you just paste in new content. Same template, consistent quality, no prompting from scratch each time.

Build a template library. They're like financial assets — you build them once and they keep working."

---

## TECHNIQUE 4: ROLE SEPARATION (9:30–11:00)

**[HOST]**

"One advanced technique: use different AI 'roles' in sequence to stress-test your work.

Example for a business idea:

1. **The Optimist:** 'You are an enthusiastic investor. Give me 5 reasons this idea could be huge.'
2. **The Critic:** 'You are a sceptical analyst. What are the 5 biggest risks and failure modes?'
3. **The Customer:** 'You are the target customer. What would make you choose this over alternatives?'
4. **The Synthesiser:** 'Given these three perspectives, what would you advise the founder to focus on first?'

You get a multi-perspective analysis that's much richer than asking a single 'is this a good idea?' question."
