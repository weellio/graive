# Capstone Project: Build Something Real
### Innovator Level (Ages 16–18) | Final Project

---

## The Brief

Build something real. Use everything from this tier.

This is deliberately open-ended — because by now you have enough knowledge and skill to define your own problem, design your own solution, and present it to a real or simulated audience.

---

## Choose Your Track

### Track A: The Working Automation
Build a functional AI automation that solves a real problem. It should:
- Run without manual input after initial trigger
- Use at least 3 connected steps (trigger → AI → action)
- Solve a problem you can describe clearly
- Be documented well enough that someone else could replicate it

**Deliverable:** Working workflow + documentation + 2-minute demo video explaining it

### Track B: The Product or Service Concept
Develop a detailed, validated concept for an AI-powered product or service.
- A one-page pitch document
- A working prototype or landing page (no-code tools allowed)
- Evidence of validation (at least 3 people you talked to about the problem)
- A 5-minute presentation you could give to a real audience

**Deliverable:** Pitch document + prototype/page + validation notes + presentation

### Track C: The Policy Brief
Write a publication-quality policy brief on an AI governance issue of your choice.
- 800–1,200 words
- Clearly structured (issue, evidence, analysis, recommendation)
- Specific and actionable recommendation
- Based on real research, verified with external sources
- Suitable for submission to a real public consultation or competition

**Deliverable:** Policy brief + a list of 3 places you could actually submit or publish it

---

## Requirements for All Tracks

### 1. Honest AI use documentation
For your project, document how you used AI:
- What did AI help you produce?
- What did you produce entirely yourself?
- Where did AI make mistakes that you had to catch?
- What did the human-AI collaboration look like?

This isn't about policing AI use. It's about being honest and reflective about the process.

### 2. The course connection
In a 300-word reflection, explain which specific concepts from this tier (advanced prompting, automation, entrepreneurship, philosophy, policy, human edge) showed up in your project. Concrete examples.

### 3. Presentation
Present your project to at least one real person — a family member, friend, teacher, mentor. Get their honest feedback. Write a one-paragraph summary of what they said.

---

## Evaluation Criteria (Self-Assess)

Rate yourself 1–5:

**Track A:**
- [ ] Automation actually works end-to-end
- [ ] Solves a real, specific problem
- [ ] Documentation is clear enough for replication
- [ ] AI prompt is intentional and well-designed
- [ ] You can explain every part of it

**Track B:**
- [ ] Problem is specific and validated
- [ ] Solution uses AI leverage intentionally
- [ ] Pitch is clear and compelling
- [ ] Prototype/landing page is real and shareable
- [ ] You could answer hard questions from a sceptical audience

**Track C:**
- [ ] Issue is well-scoped and specific
- [ ] Both sides are represented fairly
- [ ] Recommendation is concrete, not vague
- [ ] Evidence is verified with real sources
- [ ] Writing is clear, concise, and persuasive

## Graduation

After completing this capstone, you will have:

- Built something with real-world application
- Used AI as a tool in service of your own thinking
- Documented your process honestly
- Presented your work to a real audience

**The full curriculum spans four tiers and four years of intellectual development. You've been through all of it.**

---

## What Comes Next

This course ends here, but your education doesn't. A few directions worth exploring:

- **Technical depth:** Learning Python for AI API integration, understanding machine learning concepts, exploring open-source AI tools
- **Policy and governance:** AI governance fellowships, academic programmes in technology law or AI ethics
- **Building:** Finding a real problem, validating it, building a first version, getting it to real users
- **Philosophy and research:** Academic philosophy of mind, cognitive science, AI safety research

The world needs people who are *both* technically capable *and* thoughtful about consequences. That combination is rare and valuable.

Go build things. Ask hard questions. Take responsibility.
