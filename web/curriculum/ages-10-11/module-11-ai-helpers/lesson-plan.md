## Key Concepts

### AI Is Everywhere (Secretly)
Most AI doesn't look like a chat window. It's quietly running in the background of apps you use every day:

- **Voice assistants** — Siri, Alexa, Google Assistant listen for your voice and understand what you say
- **Autocomplete** — when your phone suggests the next word as you type, that's AI
- **Recommendation engines** — when YouTube suggests a video, or Spotify plays a song you like, that's AI predicting what you want
- **Spam filters** — your email app uses AI to decide which messages are junk
- **Face unlock** — your phone uses AI to recognise your face
- **Autocorrect** — AI guessing what word you meant to type
- **Search engines** — Google uses AI to decide which results to show you first

> "Every time an app seems to 'know' what you want before you ask — that's AI learning your patterns."

### How Recommendation Engines Work
When you watch a YouTube video, the AI notes:
- What you watched
- How long you watched it
- What you watched next
- What people similar to you watched

Then it recommends more videos it thinks you'll like. This is really good at keeping you watching — sometimes too good.

### Voice Assistants: How Do They Understand?
When you say "Hey Siri, what's the weather tomorrow?" — this is what happens:
1. The microphone picks up your voice
2. AI converts your speech to text
3. AI understands what you're asking (not just the words, but the *meaning*)
4. It searches for the answer
5. It converts text back to speech and reads it to you

That's four different AI jobs happening in about a second!

### Activity 2: Test a Voice Assistant (10 mins)

If you have access to Siri, Alexa, Google Assistant, or a similar tool, try these experiments:

Ask it:
1. A simple factual question: "What is the tallest mountain in the world?"
2. A weird question: "Why do cats knock things off tables?"
3. A task: "Set a timer for 2 minutes."
4. Something local: "What's the weather like right now?"
5. Something creative: "Tell me a joke about school."

Rate each answer:
1. Factual question: ___/10
2. Weird question: ___/10
3. Task: ___/10
4. Local information: ___/10
5. Creative question: ___/10

### Activity 3: The Recommendation Experiment (10 mins)

This is a thinking activity — no device needed.

Think about a streaming service or video app you use (YouTube, Netflix, TikTok, Spotify).

Answer these questions honestly:

1. Has it ever recommended something you loved that you wouldn't have found yourself? ___

2. Have you ever ended up watching/listening to something for longer than you planned because of recommendations? ___

3. Do you think the recommendations are usually things that are **good for you**, or just things the app thinks you'll click on? ___

4. Complete this sentence: "I think the recommendation AI knows that I like ___."

5. "One time the recommendation was really wrong or weird — it suggested ___."

## The Big Question: Helping or Controlling?

AI helpers make life more convenient. But they also make choices on your behalf:
- They decide which videos appear first
- They decide which search results you see
- They decide which songs play next

> "When AI chooses what you see, it's making decisions about your world. That's powerful — and worth thinking about."

Do you think AI recommendations mostly help you, or mostly distract you?

My honest answer: ___
Rating: AI recommendations are helpful ___/10 and distracting ___/10

## Reflect
1. Before this lesson, did you realise how many things around you were using AI? What surprised you most?
2. Is there anything you'd like AI to stop "helping" with? Something where you'd rather make your own choices?
3. If the recommendation AI only showed you things it thought you'd already like, would you ever discover something new? What's the problem with that?

## Challenge
**The AI-Free Hour:**

Pick one hour during your normal day and try to notice every time you *would* use something AI-powered — but choose not to, or choose a different option instead.

For example:
- Instead of asking Siri, look it up yourself
- Instead of watching what YouTube suggests, search for something specific
- Instead of letting Spotify choose, make your own playlist

Afterwards, write down:
- What was harder without AI? ___
- What was actually better without AI? ___
- What did you notice about your own choices when AI wasn't making them for you? ___
