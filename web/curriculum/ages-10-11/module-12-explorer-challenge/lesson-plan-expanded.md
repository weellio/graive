# Lesson Plan: Explorer Challenge
### Explorer Level (Ages 10–11) | Module 12

---

## About This Lesson

You have made it to the final module of the Explorer curriculum — and that is genuinely worth being proud of. You started this journey in Module 1 by discovering that AI is a pattern spotter, not a robot brain. Since then, you have learned to write powerful prompts using the Three S's and the Recipe formula. You have practised fact-checking AI and spotting hallucinations. You have built an online safety card, created original work with AI, spotted hidden AI in your daily life, understood how training data shapes AI's behaviour, mastered iterative remixing, explored AI as an assistant for your hobbies, and developed a detective's eye for spotting fake content. That is a remarkable amount of genuine knowledge and skill.

Now it is time to put it all together. This lesson is not just a review — it is a demonstration. The Explorer Challenge asks you to perform at your best, using everything you have learned, in three different contexts. Like any good assessment, it tests not just whether you can repeat what you were taught, but whether you can apply it in new situations. Can you write a powerful prompt under time pressure? Can you get AI to produce something genuinely excellent with thoughtful prompting and iteration? Can you explain a complex idea so clearly that even a 7-year-old could understand it?

One thing to keep in mind as you work through the challenge: your score matters less than your thinking. The rubrics measure observable outcomes, but what really counts is the reasoning behind your choices. Why did you word your prompt the way you did? Why did you choose that particular improvement for your second prompt? Why does your explainer work for a younger audience? These "why" questions are where the real learning lives — and if you can answer them, you have genuinely mastered the Explorer level.

After you complete the challenge, there is one more thing to do: look back. Think about who you were at the start of Module 1 — curious but probably uncertain about what AI actually is — and compare that to who you are now. You are someone who understands what AI is, how it works, what it is good at, what it gets wrong, how to use it creatively and safely, and how to keep a clear head in a world that is increasingly shaped by it. Not many people your age can say that. You earned it.

- **Applied skill:** Using knowledge in a new situation, not just repeating it — the mark of genuine understanding.

- **Self-assessment:** Honestly evaluating your own work against clear criteria — a skill used by professionals in every field.
- **Explorer curriculum mastery:** Demonstrating competence across all the major skills introduced in Modules 1–11.

## The Challenge — Three Rounds

This is the **Explorer Prompting Championship**. There are three rounds, each testing a different skill you have built. After each round, score yourself using the rubric provided.

---

### Round 1: The Speed Prompt (10 mins)

**The challenge:** Get the best possible result in exactly 3 prompts (no more, no fewer).

**Your mission:** Get AI to write a short story (150–200 words) that includes ALL of the following:
- A main character who is exactly your age
- A problem involving technology (it can be AI, phones, computers, anything)
- A surprising twist at the end
- At least one moment that makes the reader smile

You have three prompts. Use them wisely:
- Prompt 1: Your opening request
- Prompt 2: One improvement or correction
- Prompt 3: Your final polish

When you are done, read your story back. Then score it:

| Criteria | 1 point | 2 points | 3 points |
|----------|---------|---------|---------|
| Does it include all 4 elements? | 1–2 elements | 3 elements | All 4 elements |
| Is the story interesting to read? | A bit boring | Mostly good | Genuinely fun |
| Does it sound like a real story? | Very robotic | Mostly natural | Feels human |

**Round 1 Score: ___ / 9**

After scoring, write one sentence about your prompting strategy: "For my three prompts, I chose to..."

---

### Round 2: The Explainer Prompt (10 mins)

**The challenge:** Use AI to explain something complicated in a way that a 7-year-old could understand.

Choose one of these topics (or suggest your own to the AI):
- How the internet works
- Why the sky is blue
- What a black hole is
- How vaccines work
- Why we have seasons

Your task: Write a series of prompts that get AI to produce an explanation that is:
- Under 100 words
- Uses at least one fun comparison or analogy (e.g. "it is like...")
- Has no complicated words that a 7-year-old would not know
- Ends with one question that would make a 7-year-old curious to learn more

You may use as many prompts as you need this round — there is no limit. The challenge is the quality of the final result.

When you are done, read it out loud as if you were explaining it to a younger sibling or friend. Does it work?

Score it:

| Criteria | 1 point | 2 points | 3 points |
|----------|---------|---------|---------|
| Is it under 100 words? | Over 150 words | 100–150 words | Under 100 words |
| Does it use a fun comparison? | No comparison | A weak one | A great one |
| Could a 7-year-old understand it? | Unlikely | Probably | Definitely |
| Does it end with a curious question? | No | Weak question | Great question |

**Round 2 Score: ___ / 12**

After scoring, write one sentence about what was hardest about this round: "The trickiest part was..."

---

### Round 3: The Remix Championship (15 mins)

**The challenge:** Start with a deliberately bad prompt and improve it until you have something genuinely impressive.

**Starting prompt (copy this exactly):**
> "Tell me about animals."

That is your starting point — deliberately vague and boring.

Now, your task is to remix and improve this prompt using what you know about:
- Adding specific details
- Setting a tone and length
- Giving the AI a role
- Including a twist or constraint that makes it creative

You have as many remixes as you want. Keep going until you have something you are genuinely proud of.

Some ideas to push yourself:
- What if the AI wrote from the animal's point of view?
- What if it focused on one surprising animal fact that most people do not know?
- What if it was written as a poem, a quiz, a news report, or a conversation?
- What if it was about an animal that relates to something you love?

When you have your final version, write it out and label it: **"My Remix Championship Entry"**

Score it:

| Criteria | 1 point | 2 points | 3 points |
|----------|---------|---------|---------|
| Is it more interesting than "Tell me about animals"? | Slightly | Noticeably | Dramatically |
| Did you use a specific format or creative angle? | No | Somewhat | Yes, clearly |
| Would you be proud to show this to someone? | Not really | Maybe | Definitely yes |
| Did you show iteration — improving through remixes? | 1 version | 2–3 versions | 4+ versions |

**Round 3 Score: ___ / 12**

After scoring, count how many remix iterations you did: ___ remixes total.

---

### Bonus Round: The Fact-Check Sprint (10 mins)

This is an optional fourth round for anyone who wants to push their score higher — or who just loves a challenge.

Ask the AI to tell you five facts about a topic you are studying in school or something you find interesting. Get the five facts, then spend exactly 5 minutes trying to verify as many of them as you can using a search engine.

Score it:

| Criteria | Points |
|----------|--------|
| For each fact you successfully verified as correct | 2 points |
| For each fact you successfully identified as incorrect or uncertain | 2 points |
| For writing a clear one-sentence verdict for each fact | 1 point each |

**Bonus Round Score: ___ / 25**
