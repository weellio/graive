# Lesson Plan: AI and Your Hobbies
### Explorer Level (Ages 10–11) | Module 10

---

## About This Lesson

One of the most common mistakes people make with AI is thinking that it is only useful for things they do not know much about. Actually, the reverse is often true: AI is most useful — and most interesting — when you already know the subject well. That is because when you are an expert, you can instantly tell what the AI gets right and wrong. You can ask better questions. You can push back on bad advice. You can take the useful parts and ignore the nonsense. The combination of your genuine expertise and AI's broad knowledge is far more powerful than either one alone.

Think about what it means to have a hobby you really love. You know things about it that most people do not. You have spent hours watching videos, reading about it, practising it, talking to other people who love it. You have opinions about which techniques work and which ones people get wrong. You have favourite players, artists, recipes, tracks, breeds, moves, or strategies. All of that knowledge you carry around is actually a superpower when you work with AI — because it lets you be a brilliant critic of everything the AI tells you.

AI tools can act as incredibly versatile assistants for any hobby. They can generate training plans, suggest creative challenges, explain techniques, answer questions you are too embarrassed to ask a real expert, summarise advice from thousands of sources, and even quiz you on what you know. What they cannot do is replace the actual feeling of doing your hobby. AI cannot feel the satisfaction of scoring a goal, landing a technique, finishing a piece of art, or hearing music you created come together. The human joy of getting better at something you love is entirely yours.

There is also something interesting that happens when you use AI as a creative partner in your hobby: it can push you toward ideas you would not have thought of alone. Ask it to suggest an unusual painting technique you have never tried. Ask it what the most underrated skill in your sport is. Ask it for a recipe that sounds completely wrong but somehow works. AI has processed information from millions of sources and can surface the unexpected gems from all of them — the unusual ideas, the counterintuitive tips, the techniques from one domain that somehow apply to another. Your job is to be the judge of whether those gems are actually worth picking up.

---

## Key Concepts

- **Domain expertise:** Deep knowledge you have built up about a specific topic — your hobby is an area where your expertise likely exceeds the AI's practical understanding.
- **AI as assistant:** Using AI as a tool to support and extend your own work, rather than replacing your own skill and effort.
- **Cross-domain knowledge:** Insights or techniques from one field that can usefully apply to another — one of AI's strengths is connecting ideas from very different areas.
- **Critical evaluation:** Assessing AI-generated advice against your own knowledge to determine what is genuinely useful and what is inaccurate or oversimplified.
- **Personalised learning plan:** A schedule or set of goals tailored to your specific level and interests — something AI can help draft but only you can truly validate.

---

## Fun Facts

- Elite sports teams are now using AI to analyse player movement, opponent patterns, and training loads — but the best teams still rely on expert coaches to interpret the data and make the final calls.
- AI has been used to help compose music in the style of composers who died hundreds of years ago — but music critics and historians are immediately able to spot the differences from the real thing.
- A chef who used AI to help generate a new recipe said the best part was that AI suggested a flavour combination he would have dismissed as impossible — but when he actually tried it, it worked brilliantly.

## Try It — AI Meets Your Passion

### Activity 1: Test AI's Knowledge of Your Hobby (10 mins)

Before you use AI to help with your hobby, let us find out how much it actually knows.

Pick your hobby (or the one you enjoy most from the list: sport, art, gaming, music, cooking, animals, reading, coding, crafts, dance, anything else).

Now ask the AI five questions about your hobby — a mix of easy and hard. For each one, decide whether you think the AI got it right, got it wrong, or got it sort of right.

**Example questions if your hobby is football:**
1. "What are the basic rules of football?"
2. "What is the offside rule and why do people find it confusing?"
3. "Who is considered the greatest goalkeeper of all time and why?"
4. "What position should a left-footed player usually play?"
5. "What should I practise first if I want to improve my passing?"

**Example questions if your hobby is drawing/art:**
1. "What are the three primary colours?"
2. "What is perspective in drawing and how do beginners learn it?"
3. "What is the difference between watercolour and gouache paint?"
4. "How do professional illustrators plan their compositions?"
5. "What should I draw every day to get better at portraits?"

Write your own 5 questions for YOUR hobby. Ask them, and for each answer, mark it:
- Correct — I already knew this, and the AI got it right
- Mostly right — the AI had the general idea but missed something
- Wrong or weird — the AI said something I know is incorrect or odd
- New to me — this is something I did not know and I want to check it

**Total your scores.** This tells you how much you can trust AI on this specific topic.

### Activity 2: AI as Your Creative Partner (10 mins)

Now use AI to actually help you with your hobby. Choose **one** of the following missions based on your hobby:

**If your hobby is a SPORT:**
Ask AI to: "Create a 2-week training schedule for an 11-year-old who wants to improve at [sport]. Focus on skills, not just fitness. Make it fun and doable after school."

**If your hobby is ART or DRAWING:**
Ask AI to: "Give me 5 creative drawing challenges that push a beginner artist to try new things — each one should take about 20 minutes and teach a different skill."

**If your hobby is GAMING:**
Ask AI to: "I play [game name]. Help me write a short guide for beginners that explains the most important things to know in the first hour of the game."

**If your hobby is COOKING or BAKING:**
Ask AI to: "Suggest 3 recipes a kid aged 10–11 could make with adult supervision. Each recipe should use ingredients most families already have and teach a different cooking skill."

**If your hobby is ANIMALS or NATURE:**
Ask AI to: "I am interested in [type of animal or nature topic]. Create a fun quiz with 10 questions and answers that would challenge someone who already knows the basics."

**If your hobby is MUSIC:**
Ask AI to: "I want to learn [instrument or music style]. Give me a beginner's 4-week learning plan with specific things to practise each week."

**For any hobby:** If none of these fit, write your own mission prompt using this template:
> "I am 10 years old and my hobby is [HOBBY]. I want to [GOAL]. Help me by [SPECIFIC REQUEST]. Keep it practical and fun."

After getting the response, decide: Is this actually helpful? Would you use it? What would you change?

---

### Activity 3: Spot What AI Gets Wrong (10 mins)

AI is good at general knowledge, but your hobby probably has details, rules, or community knowledge that AI gets slightly wrong or oversimplifies.

Read back through the AI responses from Activities 1 and 2. Find at least **two things** that you, as a hobby expert, know are not quite right.

These might be:
- A rule it described incorrectly
- A technique that sounds odd to someone who actually does it
- Something it left out that is really important
- An opinion it stated as fact

For each one, write a **correction prompt** — a message telling the AI where it went wrong and asking it to fix it. For example:

> "Actually, in [sport], that rule only applies when [situation]. Can you correct that part of your answer?"

or

> "You said [thing], but most experienced [hobby] people would say [correct thing]. Can you update your advice to reflect that?"

This activity teaches you something really important: **AI is more useful when you know the subject well enough to catch its mistakes.**

### Activity 4: The Unexpected Challenge Generator (10 mins)

This activity uses AI to push you outside your creative comfort zone within your hobby.

Ask the AI to generate three unusual or unexpected challenges related to your hobby — things you would probably never have thought to try on your own.

Use this prompt:
> "I am interested in [YOUR HOBBY]. Give me three unusual creative challenges that would push someone who already knows the basics. Each challenge should involve doing something unexpected, combining my hobby with something unrelated, or trying a technique that is not normally used in this hobby. Keep them achievable for an 11-year-old."

Read the three challenges. Then:
1. Rate each one from 1–5 on how much you want to try it
2. Pick the one you want to try most and explain why
3. Write a short plan for actually doing it this week

**The big idea:** AI can suggest directions you would not have gone on your own. You get to decide which ones are worth exploring.

## Draw It!

Draw a scene showing you doing your hobby — but with an AI assistant floating nearby (as a robot, a helper character, a magic screen, whatever you like).

In speech bubbles, show:
- Something you are asking the AI for help with
- Something the AI gets right
- One thing you tell the AI it got wrong

Add a label at the bottom: **"I am the expert. AI is the assistant."**

Make it fun — give your AI assistant character a name if you want!

## Reflect
Answer these questions out loud or write them down:

1. Did AI know as much about your hobby as you expected? Were there any surprises — either things it knew well or things it got completely wrong?
2. Could AI ever replace a real coach, teacher, or expert in your hobby? What can a real human give you that AI cannot?
3. Now that you have seen AI work on something you love, are you more or less excited about using it? What would you use it for going forward?
4. In the Unexpected Challenge Generator activity, did any of the AI's suggestions genuinely inspire you? What does that tell you about how AI can contribute to your hobby even when it does not fully understand it?
5. If your future self became a professional in your hobby, how do you think AI might be used in that profession? Would you welcome it or be worried about it?

## Level Up Challenge

Create a complete "AI-assisted improvement plan" for your hobby.

This plan should include:
- A self-assessment: What are you currently good at? What do you most want to improve?
- A 4-week schedule of activities and practice goals (use AI to help draft this, then edit it based on your real knowledge of your hobby)
- Three specific things you will ask AI for help with over the next month
- Three things in your hobby that you will NOT use AI for — because those things require your personal skill, presence, or judgment
- A way to measure your progress at the end of 4 weeks

After creating the plan, have a real expert in your hobby (a coach, parent, older sibling, or teacher) review it. Do they think the AI's suggestions were good? What would they add or change?

This is a genuinely practical exercise — the goal is to end up with a plan you will actually use.

## Share (Optional)
Share your 2-week training plan, drawing challenges, recipe list, or whatever you created in Activity 2 with someone who shares your hobby. Ask them: "Would you actually do any of this? Is it good advice?" Their feedback will tell you a lot about how useful AI really is for your hobby.
