# Explorer Level — Ages 10–11
## Theme: Talking to Computers

---

### Who This Is For
10–11 year olds who are curious, love games, and have probably already used voice assistants or seen AI in action. They learn best through play, concrete examples, and immediate "that's so cool!" moments. Abstract concepts need anchoring in things they already know.

### The Big Idea
Computers aren't magic — and talking to them well is a skill you can learn. You're going to learn how to ask computers to do amazing things by getting really good at explaining exactly what you want.

### Core Skill by End of Tier
Write a specific, structured prompt using context + instruction + format — and improve it by trying again.

### Capstone
The Prompt Cooking Show — each student builds their best AI story or creative piece using everything they've learned, shows "before and after" prompts, and explains what made the difference.

---

### Tone & Design Notes
- Use game-show energy, challenges, and friendly competition where possible
- Every concept needs a concrete analogy tied to something 10-11 year olds know
- Keep videos under 7 minutes
- Activities should produce something visible and shareable (a story, an image description, a poem)
- Avoid jargon entirely — if a technical word is needed, explain it with an analogy first

---

### Prompt Engineering Progression: INTENTION
> "What do I actually want?" — learning that vague questions get vague answers, and being specific is a superpower.
