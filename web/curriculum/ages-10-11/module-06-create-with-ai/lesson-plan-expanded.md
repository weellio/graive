## About This Lesson

Every great piece of creative work in history started with a person having an idea. The sculptor imagined the statue before the first chip of marble fell. The film director had a vision before a single frame was filmed. The songwriter heard the melody in their head before it reached a microphone. Tools — whether a chisel, a camera, or a piano — do not create anything by themselves. They amplify what the person behind them can already imagine. AI is the most powerful creative amplifier ever invented, and in this lesson, you are the artist.

Here is something that might surprise you: AI is actually quite bad at having original ideas. When you ask it to "write a story," it produces something that feels like an average of all the stories it has ever read — competent, structurally correct, but somehow safe and predictable. The magic happens when a human with genuine creativity and specific vision directs the AI. Your weird idea, your surprising combination, your personal perspective — those are things AI cannot generate on its own. You bring the spark; AI brings the speed.

There are three creative moves that unlock AI's best output. The first is the Combination — taking two things that do not normally go together and fusing them. A cooking show that takes place on a submarine. A mystery story told from the perspective of a piece of furniture. The more unexpected the combination, the more interesting the result tends to be. The second is the Twist — taking something familiar and asking "what if?" What if dogs could talk but nobody believed them? What if the villain was right all along? Twists take ordinary material and make it memorable.

The third move is the Voice Swap — telling something from an unexpected point of view. A history lesson from the perspective of a stone that watched it happen. A recipe written by someone who has never cooked. A sports report narrated by someone who does not understand the sport at all. Voice swaps produce comedy, insight, and freshness that straight storytelling rarely manages. Together, these three moves give you an unlimited toolkit for creative work — and today you are going to use all of them.

---

## Key Concepts

- **Creative vision:** The human element of creative work — the idea, the purpose, the emotion, and the specific imaginative choices that make something original.
- **Combination prompt:** A creative technique where two unexpected things are merged to create something new.
- **Twist prompt:** A "what if?" question applied to something familiar to create a surprising or thought-provoking result.
- **Voice swap:** Writing something from an unexpected or unusual point of view to create freshness and surprise.
- **Iteration in creativity:** Improving a creative work through multiple rounds of feedback and refinement — just like any professional artist does.

---

## Fun Facts

- Professional screenwriters, novelists, and game designers are already using AI as a brainstorming tool — not to write their stories for them, but to generate options and ideas that they then choose from, improve, and make their own.
- Studies of human creativity show that the most original ideas usually come from combining things from different domains — scientists who have wide-ranging hobbies, for example, produce more breakthrough ideas. The Combination prompt mimics this naturally creative way of thinking.
- Some of the most popular AI-assisted creative work comes from people who gave very specific, unusual instructions — vague prompts produce generic results, but detailed, quirky prompts produce genuinely memorable ones.

## Try It — The Creative Studio

### Warm-Up: Try the Three Moves (15 mins)

Quickly try each creative move from the video:

**Move 1 — The Combination:**
Pick two unexpected things to combine. Write a short prompt (2–3 sentences) and run it.

My combination: _______ + _______

**Move 2 — The Twist:**
Pick something familiar and ask "what if?" Write and run it.

My twist: What if _______________________?

**Move 3 — The Voice Swap:**
Tell something from an unexpected point of view. Run it.

My voice swap: _______ from the perspective of _______

**Which of the three got the best result?**

### Main Event: Make Your Masterpiece (30–35 mins)

Now create ONE thing you're really proud of. Choose your project type:

| Option | Description |
|--------|-------------|
| Short Story | 1 page, with a character, a problem, and an ending |
| Song Lyrics | A verse and chorus about something you love |
| Fake News | A funny headline + first 3 paragraphs |
| Game Concept | A game name, how you win, one special rule |
| Product Ad | Invent a silly product and write its advertisement |
| Nature Documentary | Your pet / a family member narrated by Attenborough |

**Step 1: Write your first prompt using the Recipe**

```
CONTEXT: ___________________________________________
INSTRUCTION: _______________________________________
FORMAT: ____________________________________________
```

**Step 2: Run it. Read it. What would make it better?**

Write your improvement idea: _______________________

**Step 3: Second prompt (improve one specific thing)**

**Step 4: Run it again. What would make it even better?**

Write your improvement idea: _______________________

**Step 5: Third prompt. Final version.**

**Your final output:** (copy it in here or paste it)

### Bonus Activity: The Genre Blender (10 mins)

This activity takes your finished masterpiece and puts it through a genre blender.

Take the piece you just created and ask the AI to rewrite it in a completely different genre. Choose one of these transformations:

- Your funny piece → rewrite it as something genuinely tense and dramatic
- Your serious piece → rewrite it as a comedy sketch
- Your story → rewrite it as a news report with a headline
- Your poem → rewrite it as an instruction manual
- Your advertisement → rewrite it as a warning label

Read both versions side by side. Which one do you prefer? Is there anything from the second version that you want to steal and put back into the first?

This technique — genre blending — is used by professional authors to break out of creative ruts and find unexpected angles on their material.

---

### Bonus Activity: The Feedback Round (5 mins)

Ask the AI to critique your own final piece. Use this prompt:

> "Here is something I created with your help: [paste your final piece]. Can you give me three things that work well about it and two specific suggestions for how to make it even better? Be honest — I want real feedback."

Read the feedback. Do you agree with it? Which suggestions would you take, and which would you reject?

The ability to receive criticism and decide what to keep and what to ignore is a genuine creative skill. Practise it here.

### The Show-Off Step

Read your finished piece to someone — a family member, a friend, anyone. Watch their reaction.

Did they enjoy it? Did they laugh? Did they ask questions?

## Reflect
1. How many times did you iterate before you were happy with the result?
2. What part of the creative process was YOU and what part was the AI?
3. Did the AI surprise you in a good way at any point? What happened?
4. When you asked the AI for feedback on your own work, was it helpful? Was there anything in the feedback you disagreed with — and if so, were you right to disagree?
5. Could someone look at your final piece and tell it was made with AI help? Does that matter?

## Level Up Challenge

Create a short creative project that uses ALL THREE moves — Combination, Twist, and Voice Swap — in a single piece.

For example: A combination of two unexpected things (say, a library and a volcano) told with a twist (what if the volcano is the librarian?), written from an unusual voice (the perspective of a book that is terrified of getting too hot).

The piece should be at least 200 words. Go through at least three iterations before calling it done. At the end, write one paragraph explaining:
- What the three moves were in your piece
- Which iteration was the biggest improvement and why
- Whether there is anything in the final piece that was entirely your idea and could not have come just from AI
