# Lesson Plan: Robots vs AI — What's the Difference?
### Explorer Level (Ages 10–11) | Module 08

---

## Objective
By the end of this lesson, you'll be able to explain the difference between robots and AI, give real-world examples of each, and understand how they can work together.

## Key Concepts

### What Is a Robot?
A robot is a **physical machine** that can be programmed to do tasks in the real world. It has:
- A body (arms, wheels, legs, sensors)
- Motors and parts that move
- A way to interact with physical things

Robots can be simple (a factory arm that welds metal) or very complex (a robotic dog that can walk and jump).

> "A robot without AI is like a puppet — it can only do what it's directly told, step by step."

### What Is AI?
AI (Artificial Intelligence) is **software** — a computer program that can learn, recognise patterns, and make decisions. It doesn't need a body. It lives on computers and servers.

AI can:
- Understand language (like ChatGPT)
- Recognise faces in photos
- Recommend videos on YouTube
- Predict what word comes next in a sentence

> "AI without a robot is like a brain in a jar — very smart, but it can't pick anything up!"

### When They Work Together
When you put AI *inside* a robot, you get something much more powerful:

| Just a Robot | Just AI | Robot + AI |
|---|---|---|
| Factory arm (repeats one move) | ChatGPT (text only) | Self-driving car |
| Automatic door (sensor trigger) | Spam filter | Boston Dynamics robot dog |
| Old toy remote car | Music recommendation | Delivery drone |

The AI tells the robot *what* to do. The robot actually *does* it in the physical world.

## Try It — Spot the Difference

### Activity 1: Sort the Examples (10 mins)

Read this list of technologies. Decide if each one is:
- **R** = Robot only (physical machine, no AI)
- **AI** = AI only (software, no body)
- **R+AI** = Both robot and AI working together
- **?** = You're not sure

Write a letter next to each one:

1. A factory conveyor belt that moves at a set speed ___
2. Netflix recommending a show you'll probably like ___
3. A self-driving Tesla car ___
4. Google Translate ___
5. A robotic arm in a car factory that welds doors ___
6. A drone that can avoid obstacles on its own ___
7. Siri answering a question on your phone ___
8. A robot vacuum cleaner that maps your house ___
9. A chess computer that beats human players ___
10. An automatic vending machine ___

**Compare your answers with a friend or family member. Did you agree?**

---

### Activity 2: Draw the Combination (10 mins)

Draw a robot that uses AI to do something helpful. It can be real or completely made up.

Your robot's name: ___
What physical job does the robot body do? ___
What does the AI brain inside it decide or figure out? ___

Draw it and label:
- The body parts (wheels, arms, sensors, etc.)
- Where the AI "brain" is
- What it's doing in your drawing

---

### Activity 3: Ask an AI About Robots (10 mins)

Open your AI tool and try these questions:

1. "What are the most interesting robots that use AI today?"
2. "What can robots do that AI alone can't do?"
3. "What can AI do that a robot without AI can't do?"

Write down the most interesting thing you learned: ___

Rate how useful the AI's answers were: ___/10

Was anything it said surprising or unexpected? ___

- **Amazon warehouse robots** — use AI to navigate and pick items without bumping into each other

## Reflect
Answer out loud or in writing:

1. In your own words, what is the main difference between a robot and AI?
2. Can you think of a job where a robot would be useful but doesn't need AI — and a different job where AI is useful but doesn't need a robot body?
3. What's one thing a human can do that neither a robot nor AI can currently do as well? Why do you think that is?
