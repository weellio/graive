## The Capstone Brief

**Your challenge:** Identify a real problem in your community, school, organisation, or field of interest. Design and build an AI-powered solution. Pitch it as if presenting to investors or a panel of judges.

**Constraints:**
- The problem must be real — affecting actual people you can name or observe
- The AI component must be meaningful (not just cosmetic)
- You must test with at least 2 real users who aren't close friends or family
- You must document your design decisions and trade-offs
- You must give a 5-minute pitch presentation

---

## Key Concepts: The Full Product Cycle

This capstone walks you through the complete product development cycle:

```
Problem Discovery → Solution Design → Build → Test → Iterate → Pitch
```

Each phase draws on different modules:
- **Problem Discovery** — Module 03 (Entrepreneurship), Module 08 (AI Product)
- **Solution Design** — Module 01 (Advanced Prompting), Module 07 (Fine-Tuning), Module 10 (Multimodal)
- **Build** — Module 02 (No-Code), Module 08 (AI Product)
- **Test** — Module 08 (AI Product)
- **Legal & Ethics** — Module 04 (Philosophy), Module 09 (AI Law)
- **Pitch** — Module 11 (AI Leadership), Module 03 (Entrepreneurship)

> "A project that fails to find real users teaches you more than a theoretical project that succeeds on paper. Ship early. Learn fast. Don't protect your idea — stress-test it."

## Phase 1: Problem Discovery (60 mins)

### Step 1.1 — Find Your Problem

The most successful products solve problems their builders personally experience or deeply understand. Think about:

- What do you find genuinely frustrating or inefficient?
- What do people you know struggle with regularly?
- What processes around you are still done manually that AI could improve?
- What information is hard to access that AI could make easier?
- What decisions are being made poorly that better information could help?

Interview at least 2 people about a problem you're considering. Key questions:
- "How do you currently handle [this situation]?"
- "What's most frustrating about it?"
- "How often does this happen?"
- "What would a perfect solution look like?"

**Problem Statement:**

In one paragraph, describe the problem. Include:
- Who specifically has this problem
- How often they face it
- What they currently do about it
- Why current solutions are inadequate

My problem statement: ___

### Step 1.2 — Validate the Problem

Before building a solution, confirm this is a real problem worth solving.

Validation questions:
- Have you observed this problem first-hand? ___
- Can you name at least 3 specific people who have this problem? ___
- Would those people be relieved if the problem was solved? ___
- Is this problem frequent enough to make a solution worthwhile? ___
- Is AI actually the right tool — or is there a simpler solution? ___

Problem validation score: ___/10 (if below 7, reconsider your problem)

## Phase 4: Test (60 mins)

### Step 4.1 — Internal Testing

Before user testing, check:
- [ ] Core user journey works end-to-end
- [ ] AI output quality is acceptable on typical inputs
- [ ] Edge cases don't break the experience badly
- [ ] No obvious harmful outputs possible

**Internal test rating: ___/10**

### Step 4.2 — User Testing

Test with at least 2 people who match your target user profile (not close friends or family).

**Testing protocol:**
- Give them the product with minimal explanation
- Observe silently while they use it
- Ask afterwards: "What confused you? What worked well? Would you use this regularly?"

## Phase 5: The Pitch (30 mins preparation + 5 min delivery)

A 5-minute pitch for an AI product typically covers:

1. **The Problem** (60 seconds): Make us feel the pain. Use a specific story or scenario.

2. **The Solution** (60 seconds): What is it, in plain language? Show the demo or screenshot.

3. **The Demo** (90 seconds): Walk through the core user journey.

4. **Why AI / Why Now** (30 seconds): Why is AI the right tool? Why does this work now?

5. **What You Learned** (30 seconds): What did users tell you? What changed after testing?

6. **What's Next** (30 seconds): What would v2 look like? What would you need to take this further?
