# Lesson Plan: Data, Privacy & Your Digital Life
### Builder Level (Ages 12–13) | Module 06

---

## Objective
By the end of this lesson, you'll have conducted a personal privacy audit and made at least three intentional decisions about your digital footprint.

---

## What You'll Need
- Your smartphone (or the device you use most)
- Access to your app store and phone settings
- A notebook or Google Doc
- 40–50 minutes

---

## Watch First
Watch **Module 06: Data, Privacy & Your Digital Life**.

Key ideas: Data is the trade for free apps. You can control more than you think.

## Try It — The Digital Privacy Audit

### Part 1: App Permissions Check (15 mins)

Go to: **Settings → Apps → Permissions** (Android) or **Settings → Privacy → Permission Groups** (iOS)

For each category below, list the apps that have access:

| Permission | Apps with access | Do they need it? |
|-----------|-----------------|-----------------|
| Microphone | | |
| Camera | | |
| Location (Always) | | |
| Location (While Using) | | |
| Contacts | | |
| Calendar | | |

For each app that has access, ask: **Does this app actually need this permission to do its job?**

If not — go revoke it right now. It takes 10 seconds per app.

**How many permissions did you revoke?** _______

---

### Part 2: Account Inventory (10 mins)

Think about all the services you have accounts with. List as many as you can:

| Service | What data do they have? | Do I still use it? |
|---------|------------------------|-------------------|
| | | |
| | | |
| | | |

For any account you haven't used in more than 6 months — consider deleting it. An old unused account is a data store that still holds your information and might get breached.

**Identify 1–2 accounts to delete or deactivate.**

---

### Part 3: AI Tool Check (10 mins)

For any AI tools you use (ChatGPT, Claude, etc.) — look in the settings for:
- Training data opt-out (some have this)
- Data deletion options
- History/chat storage settings

You don't have to change anything. Just find out what settings exist.

**What did you find?** _______________________

## Level Up — Read a Privacy Policy (Sort of)

Pick one app or service you use regularly. Find its privacy policy.

You don't have to read the whole thing. Instead, use AI to summarise it:

```
I'm going to paste a privacy policy. Please summarise it in plain language for a 13-year-old.
Specifically tell me:
1. What data they collect
2. Who they share it with
3. How I can delete my data
4. Anything that seems unusual or surprising
Here is the privacy policy: [PASTE IT]
```

Copy a section of the privacy policy (the data collection section is usually near the top) and paste it in.

**What surprised you most?**

---

## Reflect

1. Before this module, how much did you think about data collection when using apps? Has your thinking changed?

2. The deal is: free app in exchange for your data. Is that a fair trade? Under what conditions?

3. Is there a difference between "nothing to hide" and "valuing privacy"? What would you say to someone who says "I don't care, I have nothing to hide"?
