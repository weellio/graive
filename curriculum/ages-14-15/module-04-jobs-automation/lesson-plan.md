# Lesson Plan: Jobs & Automation
### Thinker Level (Ages 14–15) | Module 04

---

## Objective
By the end of this lesson, you can apply the task-level automation framework to a real career, identify your most durable skill development priorities, and articulate a reasoned position on the future of work.

---

## What You'll Need
- AI tool + search engine
- Notebook or Google Doc
- 60 minutes

---

## Watch First
Watch **Module 04: Jobs & Automation**.

Key framework: It's not jobs vs AI — it's tasks. Routine cognitive/physical tasks → high risk. Non-routine judgment, relationships, creativity → lower risk.

---

## Try It — Career Autopsy

### Part 1: Choose and Research a Career (15 mins)

Pick a career that genuinely interests you — or one a family member has, or one that seems important to society.

Run this research prompt:
```
I'm doing a task-level automation analysis of [CAREER].
Please:
1. List the 8–10 main tasks someone in this job does regularly
2. For each task, rate its automation risk (High / Medium / Low) and explain why
3. Which part of this job is most likely to change in the next 10 years?
4. Which skills will become MORE valuable as automation handles routine tasks?
5. What does a version of this job might look like in 2035?
```

Create a table in your notebook:

| Task | Automation Risk | Reason |

| | | |

### Part 3: The "Both Sides" Analysis (15 mins)

Use AI to stress-test your thinking:

**Prompt 1 — The pessimistic view:**
```
I want the strongest possible argument that AI will cause serious unemployment
and economic disruption in the next 10–15 years. Don't soften it.
What's the most evidence-supported pessimistic case?
```

**Prompt 2 — The optimistic view:**
```
Now give me the strongest possible argument that AI will create more jobs
than it displaces, and that most workers will adapt successfully.
What's the most evidence-supported optimistic case?
```

After reading both:
- Which evidence did you find more convincing?
- What's the strongest point from each side?
- What would you need to know to be more certain?

## Level Up — Design Your Future-Proof Skill Stack

Design a 2-year personal development plan (age 14–16) focused on building skills that will matter in an AI-shaped economy.

Structure it as:
1. **Technical skills** (AI tools, coding basics, data literacy, etc.)
2. **Communication skills** (writing, presenting, cross-cultural)
3. **Domain expertise** (what subject area do you want to go deep in?)
4. **Human skills** (empathy, leadership, emotional intelligence)

For each area: what would you do? What resources? How would you measure progress?
