# Lesson Plan: The Digital Economy
### Thinker Level (Ages 14–15) | Module 03

---

## Objective
By the end of this lesson, you can explain how a major tech platform makes money, identify your own role in the digital economy, and evaluate a career path in the context of how AI is reshaping value.

## Try It — Follow the Money

### Part 1: Platform Business Model Research (20 mins)

Choose ONE platform you use regularly. Use AI-assisted research to answer:

**Prompt:**
```
I'm researching how [PLATFORM] makes money. Please explain:
1. Its primary revenue sources (be specific about percentages if known)
2. How AI is used in its business model
3. Who its biggest customers are (hint: usually not the users)
4. How its revenue has changed in the last 3–5 years
5. What risks could disrupt its current model
```

Then verify 2–3 key facts with a search engine.

Fill in the summary:

| Primary revenue source | |

| Main customers | |
| Biggest financial risk | |

---

### Part 2: Your Personal Economic Role (15 mins)

For the same platform, map your own role:

**As a consumer:**
- What do you get from the platform?
- What does it cost you (money / time / data / attention)?

**As a product:**
- What data does it have about you?
- How much would that data be worth to an advertiser? (Research: average revenue per user for this platform)

**As a potential creator:**
- How does this platform monetise creators?
- What would you need to do to earn £100 / month from it? (Research this specifically)

---

### Part 3: Value vs Cost Analysis (10 mins)

Write a short paragraph: Is this platform a fair deal for you personally?

Consider:
- The value you get from it
- What you give up (time, data, attention)
- Who benefits most from the transaction
- Whether you'd use it differently if you were paying £5/month instead

---

## Level Up — Creator Economy Case Study

Research one successful independent creator (YouTuber, podcaster, newsletter writer, streamer — someone real).

Use AI to help:
```
I'm researching [CREATOR NAME] as a case study in the creator economy.
Explain:
1. Their platform and niche
2. How they make money (multiple revenue streams if applicable)
3. How long it took them to reach sustainability
4. What risks they face
5. What skills made them successful beyond just their content
```

**What would YOU need to build a sustainable audience in something you care about?**

---

## Reflect

1. Before this module, did you think of yourself as a "product"? How does that framing change how you feel about using free platforms?

2. Is the attention economy making us more or less informed, entertained, and connected? Or does it depend on how you use it?

3. If you were designing a social media platform that was financially sustainable but *didn't* sell advertising, how would it work? What trade-offs would you make?
