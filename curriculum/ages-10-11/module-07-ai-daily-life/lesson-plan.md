# Lesson Plan: AI in Your Daily Life
### Explorer Level (Ages 10–11) | Module 07

---

## Objective
By the end of this lesson, you can spot at least five places where AI is already working in your daily life — on your phone, in games, through music apps, and in smart assistants — and explain what the AI is actually doing in each case.

---

## What You'll Need
- A device with internet access
- A free AI tool (ChatGPT free or Claude free)
- Paper and coloured pens OR a digital document
- A phone, tablet, or smart speaker nearby (if you have one)
- About 30 minutes

---

## Watch First
Watch **Module 07: AI in Your Daily Life** with a parent or on your own.

Remember: AI is not just robots and sci-fi. It is quietly working in hundreds of apps and gadgets you already use every single day — it just does not always announce itself.

---

## Try It — Spot the Hidden AI

### Activity 1: The AI Treasure Hunt (10 mins)

AI is hiding everywhere. Your job is to find it.

Go through this list and tick every one that applies to your life:

**On your phone or tablet:**
- [ ] My phone suggests words when I type a message
- [ ] My phone unlocks using my face or fingerprint
- [ ] A map app has ever suggested the fastest route for me
- [ ] A camera has ever made my photos look brighter or removed red-eye
- [ ] An app has ever recommended a video, song, or game to me

**In games:**
- [ ] I have played a game where enemies figure out where I am hiding
- [ ] A game has ever adjusted its difficulty because I was doing too well or struggling
- [ ] I have played against a computer opponent that felt like it was thinking

**In music and video:**
- [ ] Spotify, YouTube, or another app has ever said "you might like this"
- [ ] A voice assistant (Siri, Alexa, Google) has ever answered a question for me
- [ ] Subtitles or captions have ever appeared automatically on a video

Count your ticks. Write down your total: ______

Most people get at least 7. Every single one of those is AI doing its job.

Now pick **three** from your list. For each one, write one sentence explaining what you think the AI is doing. For example:

> "When YouTube recommends a video, I think the AI looks at what I have watched before and finds videos that are similar."

You do not need to be exactly right — just give it your best guess. You will check your thinking in Activity 3.

---

### Activity 2: Interview a Smart Assistant (10 mins)

If you have access to a voice assistant (Siri, Alexa, Google Assistant) or a chatbot like ChatGPT, this activity will show you just how much AI can understand — and where it gets confused.

Ask a smart assistant **all five** of these questions and note what happens:

1. "What is the weather like today?" — Does it know where you are?
2. "Tell me a joke." — Is the joke actually funny?
3. "What did I eat for breakfast?" — What does it say?
4. "Remind me to do my homework in one hour." — Does it understand the request?
5. "Who is my best friend?" — What does it say?

After asking all five, think about this:

- Questions 1, 2, and 4 probably worked well. Why do you think that is?
- Questions 3 and 5 probably failed or gave a strange answer. Why?

The big idea: AI assistants are good at questions that have answers in public data or in your device's settings. They cannot know personal things about your life — your breakfast, your friends, your feelings — unless you have told them.

Write two sentences: one thing the assistant was good at, and one thing it could not do.

---

### Activity 3: Check Your Guesses (10 mins)

Remember those three guesses you wrote in Activity 1? Now let's fact-check them using an AI tool itself.

Open ChatGPT or Claude and ask:

> "I am 10 years old learning about AI. Can you explain in simple words how [name the thing — e.g. Spotify recommendations] uses AI? Keep it short and easy to understand."

Do this for each of your three guesses.

Then compare:
- Was your guess correct, partially correct, or quite different?
- Did anything surprise you?

Write a short paragraph (3–5 sentences) about what you found out. Start with: "Before this lesson, I thought..."

---

## Draw It!

Draw a picture of yourself going through a single morning — from waking up to arriving at school (or sitting down at your desk at home).

Circle every moment where AI might be involved. Think about:
- Your alarm or phone
- The route to school
- Music or podcasts you listen to
- The suggestions on your apps
- Any cameras you pass

Label each circle with what the AI is doing. Try to find at least **five AI moments** in your morning.

Add a title to your picture: **"My AI-Powered Morning"**

---

## Reflect
Answer these questions out loud or write them down:

1. Before this lesson, did you think AI was a big part of your daily life? Has that changed?
2. Most of the AI you spotted was trying to be helpful. Can you think of a situation where one of those "helpful" AIs might actually be annoying or unfair?
3. Spotify and YouTube recommend things based on your past choices. What could go wrong with that? (Hint: think about whether you would ever discover anything new.)

---

## Share (Optional)
Tell someone at home about the most surprising place you found AI today. Ask them: "Did you know your phone uses AI to do that?" See if you can teach them something they did not already know.
