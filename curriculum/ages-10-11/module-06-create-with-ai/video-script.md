### Explorer Level (Ages 10–11) | Module 06 | ~7 minutes

And I'm going to show you something first that I think is going to blow your mind a little bit.

Ready? Let's go."

**[TITLE CARD: "Create with AI"]**

---

## AI AS A CREATIVE PARTNER (0:40–2:00)

**[HOST]**

"Here's something important about using AI for creative work: AI isn't replacing your creativity. It's *amplifying* it.

The idea, the direction, the taste — that's yours. You're the one deciding what makes something good. What makes it funny, or touching, or cool. AI is just a very fast, very experienced writing and idea machine.

Think of it like having a creative assistant who will try anything you suggest. They won't judge. They won't get bored. They'll keep going until you're happy.

Your job is: have good ideas, know what 'good' looks like, and keep refining until you get there.

That skill — knowing what good looks like and being able to guide someone toward it — that's called creative direction. And it's one of the skills that matters more than ever in an AI world."

---

## THE CREATIVE TOOLKIT (2:00–4:30)

**[HOST, demo]**

"Let me show you three creative moves that work really well:

**Move 1: The Combination**
Take two completely unexpected things and combine them.
- A detective story + a baking competition
- A ghost + a mobile phone
- Vikings + social media
- A superhero whose power is remembering everyone's name

Try: 'Write a short story about a Viking who discovers Instagram.'

**[Demo: show the output — it'll be funny]**

**Move 2: The Twist**
Take something familiar and add an unexpected twist.
- What if dogs were actually in charge and humans were the pets?
- What if gravity worked backwards on Tuesdays?
- What if everyone could see what song was playing in other people's heads?

Try: 'Write a world where cats have been secretly running the government for 400 years. Write a news report revealing it.'

**[Demo: show the output]**

**Move 3: The Voice Swap**
Tell a story or give an explanation from an unexpected point of view.
- A history lesson from the perspective of the losing side
- A nature documentary about humans, narrated by a pigeon
- A recipe written by someone who hates food

Try: 'Write a nature documentary narration about a human trying to operate a printer, as narrated by David Attenborough observing an alien species.'

**[Demo: show the output — it'll be brilliant]**"

---

## THE KEY MOVE: ITERATION (4:30–5:30)

**[HOST]**

"Here's the secret to getting really good creative work from AI: you don't stop after one output.

You look at what came out and ask: 'What would make this 20% better?'

Maybe it needs more humour. Maybe one character needs a stronger personality. Maybe the ending needs a twist. Maybe it needs to be shorter and punchy-er.

You keep going until you're happy. Three, four, five iterations — each one better than the last.

The best creative output is always a collaboration between your direction and AI's drafting.

**You bring the vision. AI brings the speed.**"

---

## CHALLENGE (5:30–6:20)

**[HOST, very energetic]**

"Your challenge — and this is the big one:

Create something that you're genuinely proud of. Could be:
- A short story (one page max)
- A poem about something you love
- A funny fake news headline + first paragraph
- A made-up product with an advertisement
- A game concept with rules and a name
- Lyrics for a song (about anything!)

You have to:
1. Use the Prompt Recipe (Context + Instruction + Format)
2. Iterate at least THREE times
3. End up with something you'd actually want to show someone

This is your proof that you've learned the skill. Go make something amazing.

And share it! I genuinely want to see what you create."
