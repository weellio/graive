# Lesson Plan: Explorer Challenge
### Explorer Level (Ages 10–11) | Module 12

---

## Objective
By the end of this lesson, you will have completed three prompting challenges that test everything you have learned in the Explorer curriculum — and you will walk away with your best AI creation yet, ready to share.

---

## What You'll Need
- A device with internet access
- A free AI tool (ChatGPT free or Claude free)
- Paper and coloured pens OR a digital document
- A timer (phone or clock)
- A friend, sibling, or parent to be your judge (optional but makes it more fun)
- About 45–60 minutes (this is your big finish — take your time!)

---

## Watch First
Watch **Module 12: Explorer Challenge** with a parent or on your own.

Remember: You have come a long way. You know what AI is, how to ask good questions, how to spot mistakes, how to remix and improve, and how to check what is real. This challenge is your chance to put it all together.

---

## The Challenge — Three Rounds

This is the **Explorer Prompting Championship**. There are three rounds, each testing a different skill you have built. After each round, score yourself using the rubric provided.

---

### Round 1: The Speed Prompt (10 mins)

**The challenge:** Get the best possible result in exactly 3 prompts (no more, no fewer).

**Your mission:** Get AI to write a short story (150–200 words) that includes ALL of the following:
- A main character who is exactly your age
- A problem involving technology (it can be AI, phones, computers, anything)
- A surprising twist at the end
- At least one moment that makes the reader smile

You have three prompts. Use them wisely:
- Prompt 1: Your opening request
- Prompt 2: One improvement or correction
- Prompt 3: Your final polish

When you are done, read your story back. Then score it:

| Criteria | 1 point | 2 points | 3 points |
|----------|---------|---------|---------|
| Does it include all 4 elements? | 1–2 elements | 3 elements | All 4 elements |
| Is the story interesting to read? | A bit boring | Mostly good | Genuinely fun |
| Does it sound like a real story? | Very robotic | Mostly natural | Feels human |

**Round 1 Score: ___ / 9**

---

### Round 2: The Explainer Prompt (10 mins)

**The challenge:** Use AI to explain something complicated in a way that a 7-year-old could understand.

Choose one of these topics (or suggest your own to the AI):
- How the internet works
- Why the sky is blue
- What a black hole is
- How vaccines work
- Why we have seasons

Your task: Write a series of prompts that get AI to produce an explanation that is:
- Under 100 words
- Uses at least one fun comparison or analogy (e.g. "it is like...")
- Has no complicated words that a 7-year-old would not know
- Ends with one question that would make a 7-year-old curious to learn more

You may use as many prompts as you need this round — there is no limit. The challenge is the quality of the final result.

When you are done, read it out loud as if you were explaining it to a younger sibling or friend. Does it work?

Score it:

| Criteria | 1 point | 2 points | 3 points |
|----------|---------|---------|---------|
| Is it under 100 words? | Over 150 words | 100–150 words | Under 100 words |
| Does it use a fun comparison? | No comparison | A weak one | A great one |
| Could a 7-year-old understand it? | Unlikely | Probably | Definitely |
| Does it end with a curious question? | No | Weak question | Great question |

**Round 2 Score: ___ / 12**

---

### Round 3: The Remix Championship (15 mins)

**The challenge:** Start with a deliberately bad prompt and improve it until you have something genuinely impressive.

**Starting prompt (copy this exactly):**
> "Tell me about animals."

That is your starting point — deliberately vague and boring.

Now, your task is to remix and improve this prompt using what you know about:
- Adding specific details
- Setting a tone and length
- Giving the AI a role
- Including a twist or constraint that makes it creative

You have as many remixes as you want. Keep going until you have something you are genuinely proud of.

Some ideas to push yourself:
- What if the AI wrote from the animal's point of view?
- What if it focused on one surprising animal fact that most people do not know?
- What if it was written as a poem, a quiz, a news report, or a conversation?
- What if it was about an animal that relates to something you love?

When you have your final version, write it out and label it: **"My Remix Championship Entry"**

Score it:

| Criteria | 1 point | 2 points | 3 points |
|----------|---------|---------|---------|
| Is it more interesting than "Tell me about animals"? | Slightly | Noticeably | Dramatically |
| Did you use a specific format or creative angle? | No | Somewhat | Yes, clearly |
| Would you be proud to show this to someone? | Not really | Maybe | Definitely yes |
| Did you show iteration — improving through remixes? | 1 version | 2–3 versions | 4+ versions |

**Round 3 Score: ___ / 12**
