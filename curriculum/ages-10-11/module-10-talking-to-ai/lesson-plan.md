## Key Concepts

### AI Has Memory (In a Conversation)
When you chat with an AI tool, it remembers everything in your current conversation. That means:
- You can refer back to things it said earlier
- You can say "make that shorter" and it knows what "that" is
- You can build on ideas step by step

BUT — when you start a **new** conversation, it forgets everything. Every new chat is a fresh start.

> "Think of each AI conversation like meeting someone with no memory of you. The conversation you're currently in is the only context it has."

### Follow-Up Prompts
Instead of trying to get a perfect answer in one message, you can have a back-and-forth:

- "Can you explain that in simpler words?"
- "Give me three more examples."
- "Make that shorter — just the key points."
- "What's the most interesting part of what you just said?"
- "Can you put that as a numbered list?"

This is called **iterating** — improving through rounds of feedback.

### Correcting AI Mistakes
AI makes mistakes. The good news? You can correct it!

If AI says something wrong:
- "That's not quite right — [correct information]. Can you try again?"
- "I don't think that's accurate. Can you double-check your reasoning?"
- "You said X earlier, but now you're saying Y — which is it?"

AI usually responds well to being corrected politely and clearly.

## Try It — The Conversation Builder

### Activity 1: One Message vs Many Messages (15 mins)

**Round 1: One-shot question**

Ask this question in a single message and write down the answer:

> "Tell me about the ocean."

Write the answer length (roughly): ___ sentences
Was it interesting? ___/10

**Round 2: The conversation version**

Start a NEW chat. This time, build the conversation over at least 5 messages. Use this sequence:

1. "I want to learn something fascinating about the ocean. What's the most surprising fact you know?"
2. (After reading) "That's interesting — can you tell me more about [the specific thing it mentioned]?"
3. "Why does that happen? Explain it like I'm 10 years old."
4. "Can you give me an example from real life that I could tell my friend?"
5. "Now summarise everything we talked about in exactly 3 sentences."

Write down the best thing you learned from the conversation: ___

Which approach gave you more useful information? One question or the conversation? ___

---

### Activity 2: The Correction Challenge (10 mins)

This activity teaches you to spot and correct AI mistakes.

Ask the AI a question in an area where you already know a lot — your favourite sport, a game you play, a place you've been, your pet, your hobby.

Your topic: ___

Ask: "Tell me about [your topic]."

Now carefully read the answer. Look for:
- Any facts that are wrong
- Anything that's missing something important
- Anything that sounds too general or vague

Did you find a mistake or something to improve? ___

If yes, write a correction message: "Actually, [correct information]. Can you update your answer?"

After correction, rate the improved answer: ___/10
Before correction, rate the original: ___/10

---

### Activity 3: Build a Story Together (10 mins)

Use a multi-turn conversation to co-write a short story with AI. Here's how:

Message 1: "Let's write a story together. You write the first paragraph — a young inventor discovers a strange machine in their attic. Make it exciting."

Message 2: (After reading) "Great! Now I'll add the next part:" — [YOU write 3–5 sentences continuing the story]

Message 3: "Add the next section where the machine does something unexpected."

Message 4: (Add your own twist)

Message 5: "Write an exciting ending."

After finishing, answer:
- What did the AI add that you wouldn't have thought of? ___
- What did YOUR additions bring to the story? ___
- How did the story turn out? Rate it: ___/10

## Important Tips for Good AI Conversations

- **Be specific.** Vague questions get vague answers.
- **Say what you don't want.** "Don't make it too long" or "Don't use complicated words."
- **Push back.** If something isn't right, say so. AI won't be offended!
- **Ask why.** After any answer, "Why?" or "How do you know that?" often reveals more.
- **Use "let's" language.** Saying "Let's explore..." or "Help me think through..." signals that you want a discussion, not just an answer.

## Challenge
**The 10-Message Conversation:**

Pick any topic you're genuinely curious about — something from school, a hobby, a mystery of the universe, anything.

Have a conversation with AI that lasts at least **10 messages** — with you actively shaping the direction the whole time.

Rules:
- Never just say "okay" or "cool" — always respond with a question, a correction, or a new direction
- Try to end up somewhere genuinely surprising or interesting
- At the end, write a 3-sentence summary of what you learned

Topic I chose: ___
Most surprising thing I learned: ___
Conversation quality rating: ___/10
