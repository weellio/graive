# Lesson Plan: What Could AI Do in Your Future?
### Explorer Level (Ages 10–11) | Module 12

---

## Objective
By the end of this lesson, you'll be able to describe how AI might change jobs and daily life in the future, identify things that humans will always do better than AI, and feel excited and prepared (not scared) about growing up in an AI world.

## Key Concepts

### AI Is Getting Better Very Fast
When your parents were your age, there was no Siri, no YouTube recommendations, and no AI that could write stories or make pictures. All of that happened in just the last 10–15 years.

By the time you're working in a job, AI will be even more powerful. That might sound scary — but it also means amazing opportunities.

> "Every new technology in history — the printing press, electricity, the internet — changed jobs and created new ones. AI is the next big one."

### Jobs AI Is Already Changing
AI is already helping with (and sometimes replacing parts of):
- Writing and editing (news summaries, reports)
- Customer service (chatbots)
- Medical diagnosis (spotting diseases in X-rays)
- Music and art creation
- Data analysis and research
- Driving and delivery

### Things Humans Will Always Do Better
Even the most advanced AI has limitations. Humans are still far better at:
- **Caring for people** — being a real friend, comforting someone who is upset
- **Physical creativity in the real world** — playing live music, acting on stage, making food with love
- **Making complex ethical decisions** — judging what's *fair* and *right* in messy situations
- **Leading and inspiring others** — motivating a team, being a role model
- **Understanding things no one has seen before** — true original discovery
- **Building trust** — a robot doctor can scan you, but can it really understand your fear?

### What You Can Do to Get Ready
The best preparation isn't to worry — it's to build skills that AI struggles with:
- Critical thinking (questioning, analysing)
- Creativity in new and unexpected ways
- Human connection (empathy, communication)
- Learning how to use AI tools well
- Combining knowledge from different areas in new ways

## Try It — Future Explorer Activities

### Activity 1: Jobs of the Future Brainstorm (10 mins)

Many jobs that will exist when you grow up haven't been invented yet. AI will create new jobs just as it changes old ones.

Here are some new job ideas that might exist in 20 years:
- AI trainer (someone who teaches AI to behave correctly)
- Digital wellbeing coach (helping people have healthy relationships with tech)
- Space habitat designer
- Climate restoration engineer
- Robot ethicist (deciding what robots should and shouldn't do)
- Virtual reality therapist

Now invent your own! Think of a problem in the future world that will need solving.

**My future job idea:**
Name of the job: ___
What problem does it solve? ___
Does it use AI? How? ___
Could AI do this job by itself? Why/why not? ___

---

### Activity 2: What Will AI Do in 20 Years? (10 mins)

Ask your AI tool these questions and write the most interesting answers:

1. "What jobs do you think will change the most because of AI in the next 20 years?"
2. "What do you think humans will always be better at than AI?"
3. "What's one thing AI will probably never be able to do?"

Most interesting answer: ___

Do you agree with what it said? ___

Rating — how useful was AI at answering questions about AI's own future? ___/10

---

### Activity 3: Your Future Vision (15 mins)

Imagine the world in 20 years — when you're in your late 20s or early 30s. Think about what a typical day might look like.

Draw OR write a description of your imagined future day. Include:
- What do you do for work (or is it something you haven't heard of yet)?
- What does your home look like?
- How do you get around?
- What does AI do for you in a normal day?
- What do you still prefer to do yourself, without AI?

**My future day: ___**

After drawing/writing, answer:
- Does your vision feel exciting, worrying, or both? ___
- What's the best part of your imagined future? ___
- What's one thing in the future you'd want to prevent or change? ___

## The Human Superpower List

You have abilities AI doesn't — and many of them are things you're already building right now:

| Your superpower | Why AI struggles with it |
|---|---|
| Empathy — truly feeling what others feel | AI can simulate empathy but doesn't experience emotion |
| Creative play and imagination | AI remixes what it knows; humans invent truly new things |
| Physical skills — sport, music, dance, cooking | AI can't live in a body and feel what movement feels like |
| Understanding fairness and justice | Ethics requires lived human experience, not just patterns |
| Making someone feel genuinely understood | Real human connection is irreplaceable |

Add your own superhuman skill that you think AI will never master:
My skill: ___
Why AI can't have it: ___

## Reflect
1. What feeling comes up most when you think about AI in your future — excitement, worry, curiosity, something else?
2. What's one thing you want to get really good at that you think will always be valuable — even in an AI world?
3. If you could give AI one superpower it doesn't have now, what would it be and why?

## Challenge
**Letter to Future You:**

Write a letter to yourself to open in 10 years (when you'll be around 20–21).

In the letter, include:
- What you understand about AI right now
- What you're curious or worried about
- What skills you want to build
- One question you have about the future that you hope to know the answer to by then
- One thing you hope never changes, no matter how powerful AI gets

Keep the letter somewhere safe — or ask a parent to email it to you to open later!
