### Explorer Level (Ages 10–11) | Module 05 | ~6 minutes

## THE FIVE ONLINE RULES (0:35–4:30)

**[HOST, one rule at a time with simple icons]**

**Rule 1: Keep Your Private Information Private** 🔒

Private information includes:
- Your full name
- Your home address
- Your school name
- Your phone number
- Your passwords
- Photos that show where you live

You wouldn't shout these things in a crowded shopping centre to a stranger. Don't share them online with people you don't know in real life either.

This applies to AI tools too. Don't type your address, phone number, or anything very personal into AI chat boxes — you don't always know where that information goes.

---

**Rule 2: Not Everyone Online Is Who They Say They Are** 🎭

The internet lets anyone present themselves however they want. Someone who says they're a 12-year-old who loves the same games might be — or might not be.

This doesn't mean everyone is dangerous. But it does mean: be cautious about sharing personal info with online friends, and always tell a trusted adult if something feels weird or makes you uncomfortable.

---

**Rule 3: Think Before You Post** 💭

Once something is online, it can be very hard to fully remove. Screenshots exist. Archives exist.

Before you post anything — a photo, a comment, a message — ask: 'Would I be comfortable if my parents, teacher, or future self saw this?'

If the answer is no or maybe — don't post it.

---

**Rule 4: If Something Feels Wrong, Tell Someone** 🗣️

If you see something online that upsets you, scares you, or makes you feel uncomfortable — tell a trusted adult. Parent, guardian, teacher, older sibling.

You won't get in trouble for telling. And getting help is always the right move.

---

**Rule 5: AI Has Rules Too** 🤖

AI tools are designed to be helpful and safe. They have rules built in — they won't help with things that could hurt people, and they're designed to be appropriate for kids.

But AI tools work best with honest, genuine questions. You don't need to try to trick them or find ways around their rules. Just use them for what they're good at — learning, creating, exploring ideas."

---

## THE AI-SPECIFIC BIT (4:30–5:15)

**[HOST]**

"One more thing that's specific to AI tools:

The conversations you have with AI might be stored or used to improve the AI. That's totally normal — but it's a reason not to type very personal stuff.

Don't type:
- Your passwords (obvious, but worth saying)
- Personal health information
- Detailed information about family problems or personal struggles

It's fine to use AI to talk through ideas, get creative help, or learn things. Just keep it as things you'd be comfortable with others seeing."

## CHALLENGE (5:15–5:40)

**[HOST]**

"Your challenge this week: make a personal Online Safety card.

Draw or write the Five Rules in your notebook. Decorate it. Put your name on it.

Then ask an adult in your life: 'What's one online safety rule you think is really important?' Add it to your card.

Keep it somewhere you'll see it."
