# Video Script: Prompts Are Like Recipes
### Explorer Level (Ages 10–11) | Module 03 | ~6 minutes

---

## HOOK (0:00–0:35)

**[HOST, holding up a recipe card and a blank piece of paper]**

"Which of these is more useful if you want to bake a cake?

**[Hold up blank paper]** — 'Make cake.'

**[Hold up recipe card]** — 'Mix 200g flour, 2 eggs, 100g sugar, 80g butter, bake at 180°C for 25 minutes.'

Obviously the recipe! Because it has all the ingredients.

Guess what? Writing a good prompt is exactly the same thing. A good prompt has ingredients too — and today I'm going to teach you the recipe."

**[TITLE CARD: "Prompts Are Like Recipes"]**

---

## THE PROMPT RECIPE (0:35–2:30)

**[HOST, ingredient cards appearing one at a time]**

"Every great prompt has three ingredients — just like every good recipe has ingredients:

**Ingredient 1: CONTEXT** 🥚
What's the situation? What does the AI need to know to help you?
*'I'm 10 years old and writing a story for school.'*
*'I have a hamster named Biscuit who loves running on his wheel.'*
*'I'm trying to explain photosynthesis to my little sister who is 6.'*

**Ingredient 2: INSTRUCTION** 🧁
What exactly do you want the AI to DO?
*'Write a funny story about what he gets up to at night.'*
*'Explain photosynthesis in simple words with a fun analogy.'*
*'Make a list of ten unusual names for a hamster.'*

**Ingredient 3: FORMAT** 🍰
How do you want the answer? What should it look like?
*'Write it as a list.'*
*'Make it exactly 5 sentences.'*
*'Use simple words that a 6-year-old would understand.'*
*'Write it like a news article.'*

**Mix all three together, and you get a recipe prompt!**"

---

## DEMO: BUILD A RECIPE PROMPT (2:30–4:30)

**[HOST, screen share or write on screen]**

"Let me show you how to build one. I want to ask AI to help me write something fun for my friend's birthday card.

**Just the instruction:**
> 'Write a birthday card message.'
**[Show generic output — bland]**

**Now with all three ingredients:**

> CONTEXT: I have a best friend called Zara. She's turning 11 and she loves dogs and video games. We always make each other laugh.
> INSTRUCTION: Write a funny birthday card message from me to her.
> FORMAT: Keep it short — 3-4 sentences. Make it feel like something a real 11-year-old would write, not an adult.

**[Show output — warm, funny, personal]**

Now THAT is a birthday card message!

Let me show you another one. I want to understand something for school:

**Just the instruction:**
> 'Explain the water cycle.'

**[Show textbook-dry output]**

**With the recipe:**
> CONTEXT: I'm 10 and I understand clouds and rain already, but I don't get how water gets back up into the sky.
> INSTRUCTION: Explain the water cycle, focusing on the part I don't understand.
> FORMAT: Use a story or analogy — like the water droplet is a character going on a journey.

**[Show engaging, accessible output with a character]**

See the difference?"

---

## CHALLENGE (4:30–5:30)

**[HOST, energetic]**

"Your challenge: use the recipe to write the BEST possible prompt for something you actually want help with.

Here are some ideas:
- A story about something you love
- An explanation of something confusing from school
- A letter, card, or message for someone
- Ideas for something you're planning

Remember: Context + Instruction + Format.

Make the prompt, run it, and see how it goes. Then try improving just ONE ingredient and run it again. Did it get better?

Share your recipe prompt in the comments — I want to see what you made!"
